# Brutally Honest Solo Dev GTM Strategy for RevealUI

**Reality Check:** Most solo dev SaaS products fail. 90% don't make $5k/month. But if you're exceptional at sales/marketing/engineering, you have a shot. This strategy assumes you'll work 60-80 hours/week for 12-18 months minimum.

## HARSH TRUTHS FIRST

1. **Your Pricing Was Way Too High** - $997+ is fantasy. Developers are cheap and skeptical.
2. **Free Competition Kills Paid Products** - PayloadCMS/Strapi are free and good enough for most.
3. **100 Customers in Year 1 Is Delusional** - Aim for 20-30 real customers in year 1.
4. **Marketing Takes 80% of Your Time** - Engineering is the easy part.
5. **Most Customers Won't Pay Without Proof** - You need testimonials, case studies, demos.

## REALISTIC PRICING (That People Will Actually Pay)

### Current Market Reality:
- Premium boilerplates: $99-$299
- SaaS templates: $49-$149
- White-label CMS: $3k-$15k/year (enterprise)
- Developer tools: Cheap/free mentality

### Your New Pricing Structure:
```
Starter:    $97 (basic CMS, limited features, community support)
            → For hobbyists, learning, small projects

Professional: $197 (full CMS + AI features, email support, 1 year updates)
            → For freelancers, indie makers, small agencies

Agency:     $397 (unlimited usage, white-label, priority support, custom features)
            → For established agencies, SaaS builders
```

**Why This Works:**
- 5x cheaper than your original pricing
- Matches what developers actually pay
- Clear upgrade path
- Can be justified by "source code access" vs hosted solutions

## YOUR NICHE: "White-Label CMS Platform for Digital Agencies"

**Why This Niche?**
- Agencies have budget (unlike individual developers)
- They need white-label solutions for clients
- Multi-tenant architecture gives them reseller capabilities
- Your AI + real-time features differentiate from basic CMS
- Competition exists but at $3k-$15k/year pricing

**Target Customer Profile:**
- Digital agencies with 5-50 employees
- Building client websites/portals regularly
- Need customizable, white-label solutions
- Open to paying for premium tools that save time

## BOOTSTRAP FUNDING: Consulting First, Product Second

### Month 1-6: Consulting to Fund Development ($150/hour target)

**Services to Offer:**
1. **Custom CMS Development** - Build client sites using your framework
2. **Headless CMS Setup** - Help agencies migrate from WordPress/Squarespace
3. **E-commerce Integration** - Stripe + CMS for client stores
4. **AI Feature Implementation** - Add chatbots, content generation to existing sites

**Revenue Target:** 20 hours/week = $3,000/week = $12,000/month
**Use for:** Product development, marketing, tools

**Finding Clients:**
- LinkedIn outreach to agencies
- Twitter/X networking
- Freelance platforms (Upwork, but mark up heavily)
- Referrals from your network

**Goal:** 5 consulting clients in first 3 months, use them as beta testers and testimonials.

## RISKS & CONTINGENCY PLANS

### Risk: No Product-Market Fit
**Signs:** <10 customers after 6 months despite marketing
**Plan:** Pivot to pure consulting business or different product

### Risk: Competition
**Signs:** Similar products launch at lower prices
**Plan:** Double down on your unique features (source code, AI integration)

### Risk: Burnout
**Signs:** Working 80+ hours/week consistently
**Plan:** Set boundaries, hire freelancers for marketing ($20-50/hour)

### Risk: Cash Flow Problems
**Signs:** Can't pay bills after 3 months
**Plan:** Keep day job/freelancing until $10k MRR, cut expenses ruthlessly
